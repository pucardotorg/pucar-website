DRISTI

The operating system for people-centric courts.

DRISTI, the Dispute Resolution Intelligent System for Transformation, is the open-source platform that powers PUCAR’s 24x7 Open and Networked Courts. It is not a digitisation of paper processes: it redesigns the court journey end to end, so that filing, scrutiny, summons, bail, hearings and orders all work the way you would build them today, natively digital, rule-based, and connected to the institutions around the court.

Built in the open as a Digital Public Good, DRISTI is PUCAR’s contribution to Phase III of India’s eCourts project, which calls for exactly this: open, interoperable infrastructure, shared data standards, and reimagined processes. A litigant should be proactively informed, a lawyer should file in minutes from anywhere, a judge should act on structured information, and none of them should run pillar to post.

Five levers of transformation

01

Integrated

The court is digitally connected to police, post and treasury. Summons, fees and processes move between institutions in real time, with tracking.

02

Asynchronous

Key case actions happen without everyone standing in the same room at the same time. Cognizance, submissions and many hearings proceed on their own clock.

03

Proactive

The court reaches out first: SMS alerts tell litigants where their case stands and what to do next, instead of leaving them to chase updates.

04

Assisted

Rule-based guidance helps lawyers file complete cases in about 30 minutes, helps staff scrutinise them, and helps judges act on structured information.

05

Data driven

A live public dashboard shows how the court is performing, stage by stage, so processes and policies evolve on evidence rather than anecdote.

The platform

From 1.0 to 2.0.

The first generation proved the model in a real court. The second is being rebuilt around everything that court taught us.

1.0 Live in Kollam

The proven foundation.

Built as a Digital Public Good on the DIGIT platform: a rules-based system that runs the 24x7 ON Court today, wired into police, post, treasury and SMS.

  • Rules-based workflows, end to end
  • Shared registries and open data standards
  • Modular services on DIGIT
  • Open source, in the commons

2.0 In build

The AI-native rebuild.

2.0 starts from the lessons of running a real court: intelligence woven through the platform rather than rules bolted on, and a system light enough to live on the court’s own hardware.

  • AI-native from the first line of code
  • Standards-compliant by default
  • Lightweight: one deployable, on-premise
  • Bespoke to each court, configurable by its own staff

Where it runs

One court live. More on the way.

ON Courts in Kollam is the first running instance of DRISTI. Work with new High Courts is underway; each state lights up here as it goes live.

24x7 ON Court, Kollam

Live since 20 November 2024 under the High Court of Kerala. It accepts cheque dishonour cases under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act arising from nine police stations in Kollam district, and it never closes: a court that goes to people, instead of people going to courts.
