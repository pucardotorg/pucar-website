About the fellowship

The People-Centric Courts initiative embeds fellows with partner courts to redesign everyday processes that create the most friction for litigants.

What you'll do

  • Shadow one process end-to-end and document where time and trust are lost
  • Co-design improvements with judges, registry staff, and lawyers
  • Publish the playbook openly so other courts can adopt it

Who this suits

  • Law graduates or practitioners with 1–5 years of experience
  • Strong writing; patience for institutional change
  • Based in, or willing to relocate to, the partner court's city

How it works

This is a paid, fixed-term fellowship stipended through a PUCAR collective member organisation. Cohorts start quarterly.
